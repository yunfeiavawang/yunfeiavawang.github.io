    Research

    My research topics focus on online support, health information diffusion, social capital on social media, and privacy management. I tried to answer the following questions: What systematic and heuristic cues influence the network structure of help-seeking information diffusion? How do users balance self-disclosure and privacy concerns on social networking sites? I am currently curious about: How to measure social capital with reply, co-occurrence, and following network and how will it influence online support receiving? I plan to explore the accumulation and transition of social capital in online support in computer-mediated environments during my graduate years.

    Methodologically, I concentrate on computational and qualitative methods, looking for ways of retrieving and analyzing social media data to expand our understanding of communication theories such as gatekeepers, weak ties, and social penetration. My past and ongoing research have utilized moderation analysis, social network analysis, content analysis, and surveys.




    Retweeting Matters! Investigating The Diffusion Of Help-seeking Messages On Social Media
    Yunfei Wang*, Shiyi Li,
    ICA 2023
    Project Page / Code

    We analyzed 836 Sina Weibo posts sent during the city-wide lockdown in Shanghai, China, in April 2022. Results show that content cues in retweets facilitate the diffusion depth of help-seeking messages. Furthermore, the content cues and sender cues in retweets have interactive effects on the diffusion depth, which calls for understanding Heuristic-systematic Model (HSM) in an interactive way under computer-mediated environments. Implications of the findings may provide assistance for future online help-seekers and help-offering users.
